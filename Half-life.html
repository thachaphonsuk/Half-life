The Basics: Atoms and Radioactivity

Before we continue, let's review what an atom is and why some atoms are unstable and emit radiation.

Atom ‚öõÔ∏è

Everything is made of atoms! Imagine an atom is like a tiny solar system. At the center is the Nucleus (like the sun), and orbiting around it are Electrons (like planets).

Unstable Atoms = Radioactive! ‚ò¢Ô∏è

Some atoms have an "unstable" nucleus because they have too much energy. To become stable, these atoms release energy in the form of radiation. This process is called Radioactive Decay.

What is Half-Life?

This is the most important concept of this lesson!

Half-Life is the time it takes for half of the radioactive atoms in a sample to decay.

Real-World Application: A Detective of Time üïµÔ∏è

Scientists use half-life to determine the age of ancient objects, like fossils! They use an element called Carbon-14, which has a half-life of 5,730 years. When a living thing dies, the Carbon-14 in its body starts to decay. Scientists can then measure the remaining amount to calculate its age. It's like a "radioactive clock".
